Meet Your Coach.

Welcome! My name is Jonathan Sturcken, and I'm proud to call the Northshore of Lake Pontchartrain, just outside New Orleans, Louisiana, my home. My lifelong passion for the arts led me to study at Southeastern Louisiana University and Berklee College of Music. Since returning to the New Orleans area in 2013, I've had the privilege of serving as a vocal educator, director, clinician, and adjudicator, witnessing firsthand the incredible wealth of talent in our community.

You may also recognize me from WYLK The Lake 94.7FM, where I hosted Music with the Maestro, a weekly radio show highlighting new and emerging artists. During the pandemic in 2020, I deepened my training through Voice Health Education (London) and became one of the first educators in the Gulf South region to be certified in Vocal Health First Aid. This new specialized training allows me to help singers make sustainable choices and address the physical, mental, and environmental factors that shape vocal health. When challenges arise, I can assess dysfunction or imbalance, support self-management, or connect clients with trusted rehabilitation and clinical specialists.

In addition to my work as a vocal coach, I have served extensively as a Music Director for a variety of theatrical productions, collaborating with performers to achieve their best vocal and musical outcomes. In 2024, I returned to Berklee to pursue continuing education in Audio Engineering and Sound Design for live events, expanding the tools I can bring to my clients in theater and live performance settings. I also continue my specialized study of voice through the Voice Study Centre (Suffolk, England) where I train with leading experts in the field of vocal health, performance, and pedagogy.

In 2025, I was honored to be named St. Tammany Parish's Performing Artist of the Year, as part of the Parish's Presidential Arts Awards. Presented by the Parish President and the Commission on Cultural Affairs, this recognition celebrates artists who contribute to the cultural vitality of our community. It's a privilege to continue sharing my passion for the arts with the region I call home.

My Approach.

Every voice is as unique as the individual who wields it. That belief lies at the heart of my teaching. I find joy in working with both aspiring and professional artists, creating a supportive, judgment-free environment that empowers each singer to explore their full potential.

My coaching is grounded in the Biopsychosocial model, which recognizes the interconnectedness of biology, psychology, and environment in shaping vocal performance. This holistic perspective helps singers connect with their instrument—their voice—in a way that is authentic, sustainable, and expressive.

I know that singing is deeply personal, and vocal challenges can sometimes feel discouraging. My philosophy is to approach those challenges with empathy, encouragement, and resilience—never at the expense of a performer's spirit. Whether you're seeking to refine technique, build confidence, or overcome hurdles, I'll meet you where you are and guide you forward.

Let's uncover the potential of your unique voice—together.
